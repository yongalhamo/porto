Project III: P.S. New York

October 20, 2021

Introduction

Education is or can be the foundational groundwork of a person’s material success. Bearing this thought in mind , I found it interesting how growing up in New York City we all attended schools within the zones of neighborhood that you lived in. Though there is the idea of convenience, I found that this sort of system makes it really easy to reinforce in-class mentality and perpetuate existing biases.

Given the city system of zoning, In this project, I intend to explore high schools , theirs neighborhoods and respective performances and also dive into fundings for given schools and analyze how it may or may not affect student performances. In addition,I will also explore how neighborhood income can be another(one of many) contingent factor in determining the success rate of a student. Understanding factors that influence a students success rate is vital in order to identify schools needing more help .

Data

NYC DOE data: List of Public schools in New York City with performance records etc.

NYC school funding Data: Funding data on Public schools in New York city

Census Data : Median Household Income by region in New York City.

Analysis After cleaning the primary data, NYC DOE data, and merging it with the other datasets (funding and neighborhood), I tried to observe the trend in graduation rate across the boroughs . Below is a visualization of all the high schools in New York City.

Initially, when I was observing the trend across the whole dataset, I could not find any trend of graduation across the boroughs. Interestingly, when I took subsets of data, segments of the highest and lowest graduation rates, and then observed trends across boroughs, there were some distinctive trends to note.

In the lowest graduation subset of the data, there is distinctively a higher count of high schools in the Bronx. Similarly in the highest graduation subset of the dataset, The Bronx has the lowest count of schools.

Similar to the comparison across the boroughs, when I first observed trend across the whole dataset across household income brackets, there was nothing that stood out but when the data was divided along the higher and lower graduation rates , there were obvious indications.

Neighborhoods with median household income of less than 45k had the highest count of schools with low graduation rates. Inversely, Neighborhoods with high income had the highest count of high schools with high graduation rate.

Given these observations , it is apparent that there is a likely correlation in a students success rate and neighborhoods/ Boroughs. Using the above method of using data subsets, below is an observation of funding across the two subsets of data and its interactions.

We can observe that Total funding and funding per pupil is generally higher for schools with higher graduation rate.

At this level of our analysis, we have observed that there seems to be a relation with graduation rate and factors such as the Borough, family income and governmental funding for the schools. Given that, I have identified and isolated schools with low graduation and college career rates, that is located in lower income neighborhoods.

These schools could use additional assistance from the government, be it in terms of funding or educational programs. The idea of equal funding needs more in depth analysis , for students from different background face very different opportunities and challenges within their personal life.

Further steps

Additional data and analysis could help provide a more accurate picture of the situation. These analysis could be enhanced in their utilization by engineering solutions and using predictive models to deduce what sort of leanings in solutions could best help students from certain backgrounds achieve some semblance of equality.
