Introduction

Being an Anime nerd,I spend more time on anime sites than all other sites combined.while on these sites, I cant help but wonder why some anime, that in my opinion are super lukewarm get scored so highly. There are numerous contingent factors that determine the score of an anime , and in this project, I will attempt to dive into the data and find out which features best contributes to the final rank/score on the anime.

Data

The data used for this project was scraped from myanimelist using BeautifulSoup.

A thousand anime titles along with their corresponding information was extracted. To avoid bias in the data, while scraping, I alternatively skipped a page , since the list was arranged in rank increasing order. This created a higher variety in my dataset.

Exploratory Data Analysis

While processing the dataset, I noticed that certain features such as Genre and Studio of the anime had too many unique values, therefore within those features, I selected values that had a frequency count below their respective set threshold for frequency and allotted them into a new variable called “Others”, like so.

Missing values were first observed within features and replaced according to each feature. Any feature using mean or median to fill missing values were held off till post train-test split since that could cause data leakage during the modeling phase.

The relation of features to rank(variable to predict), were then cumulatively observed under their categories of either being a Numerical feature, categorical feature or a dummy variable . The impact of each feature value on rank, gives a broad understanding and overview of the dataset and its relation to the variable to the target variable.

Due to the dummies from the categorical features, there was a huge increase in dimension of the dataset, therefore before implementing a baseline , I tried Lasso regression, random forrest and K-best for feature selection.

K-best had the best results in the baseline implementation of a Linear regression.

Model Evaluation and Selection For Modeling , I first implemented the baseline Linear Regression , then went on to standardize and then model Ridge and Lasso.

All of the models performed similarly and poorly on train and validation set. average Cross validation score was even worse. Entertaining the idea of over fitting , despite it fitting really poorly to the train set, I attempted binning using the KBinsDiscretizer library and polynomial transformation.
